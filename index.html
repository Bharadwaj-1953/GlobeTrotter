About the Project

GlobeTrotter is a travel planning system designed for college students. The goal of the system is to simplify how students search, compare, and plan trips without switching between multiple platforms.

The system allows users to explore travel options based on budget, preferences, and constraints, compare different plans, and make informed decisions. It also supports group planning and itinerary management, making the overall experience more structured and efficient.

User Research

We conducted semi-structured interviews with six college students to understand how they currently plan trips and the challenges they face. Participants included both undergraduate and graduate students with varying levels of travel experience.

Students reported that planning trips requires switching between multiple platforms such as airline websites and booking apps. This process is time-consuming and makes it difficult to compare options. Group travel adds additional complexity when balancing preferences, cost, and schedules.

Key Problems

  • Switching between multiple platforms
  • Slow and unstructured group decision-making
  • Difficulty comparing cost and time trade-offs
  • Planning feels overwhelming

User Needs

  • Compare options in one place
  • Clear cost and time information
  • Support for group coordination
  • Simple and guided workflows

Storyboards

Simple Task – Budget Trip Planning

A student searches for travel options within a fixed budget, compares available plans, and selects the best option.

Moderate Task – Comparing Travel Options

Users compare multiple travel options and evaluate trade-offs between cost and travel time before making a decision.

Complex Task – Itinerary and Events

Users manage their itinerary, explore nearby events, compare options, and add selected activities to create a complete travel plan.
