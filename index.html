Project Introduction

GlobeTrotter is a travel planning system designed for college students. The goal of the system is to simplify how students search, compare, and plan trips without needing to switch between multiple platforms.

The system allows users to explore travel options based on budget and preferences, compare plans, and make informed decisions. It also supports group planning and itinerary management, helping users organize their trips more efficiently.

User Research

We conducted semi-structured interviews with six college students to understand how they currently plan trips and what challenges they face.

Most students reported that they rely on multiple platforms such as airline websites, hotel booking apps, and search engines. This makes the process time-consuming and difficult when comparing options. Group travel planning adds additional complexity when trying to balance different preferences, budgets, and schedules.

Key Problems

  • Switching between multiple platforms
  • Slow and unstructured group decision-making
  • Difficulty comparing cost and time trade-offs
  • Planning feels overwhelming

User Needs

  • A single place to compare travel options
  • Clear cost and time information
  • Support for group planning and coordination
  • Simple and guided planning workflows

Storyboards

Simple Task – Budget Trip Planning

A student searches for travel options within a fixed budget, compares available plans, and selects the best option.

Moderate Task – Comparing Travel Options

Users compare multiple travel options and evaluate trade-offs between cost and travel time before making a decision.

Complex Task – Itinerary and Events

Users manage their itinerary, explore nearby events, compare options, and add selected activities to create a complete travel plan.
