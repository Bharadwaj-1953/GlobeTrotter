About the Project

GlobeTrotter is a travel planning system designed for college students. The goal of the system is to simplify how students search, compare, and plan trips without switching between multiple platforms.

The system allows users to explore travel options based on budget, preferences, and constraints, compare different plans, and make informed decisions. It also supports group planning and itinerary management, making the entire travel process more organized and efficient.

User Research

We conducted semi-structured interviews with six college students to understand how they currently plan trips and what challenges they face. Participants included both undergraduate and graduate students with different levels of travel experience.

The findings showed that students often rely on multiple platforms such as Google, airline websites, and booking apps to gather information. This process is time-consuming and makes it difficult to compare options effectively. Group travel planning adds another layer of complexity, especially when trying to balance preferences, cost, and schedules.

Key Problems

  • Users switch between multiple platforms to plan a single trip
  • Group decision-making is slow and unstructured
  • Comparing options across cost and time is difficult
  • Planning feels overwhelming and time-consuming

User Needs

  • A single platform to compare travel options
  • Clear visualization of cost and time trade-offs
  • Support for group coordination and voting
  • Simple and guided planning workflows

Storyboards

Simple Task – Budget Trip Planning

A student searches for travel options within a fixed budget, compares available plans, and selects the best option to save. This task focuses on quick decision-making with minimal complexity.

Moderate Task – Comparing Travel Options

Users compare multiple travel options and evaluate trade-offs between cost and travel time. This task introduces decision complexity where users balance different constraints.

Complex Task – Itinerary and Events

Users manage their itinerary, explore nearby events, compare options, and add selected activities to create a complete travel plan. This task reflects real-world planning beyond initial booking.
