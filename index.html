About the Project

GlobeTrotter is a travel planning system designed for college students. The goal of the project is to simplify how students search, compare, and plan trips without switching between multiple platforms.

The system supports budget-based planning, comparison of travel options, group decision-making, and itinerary management. It is designed to make the travel planning process more organized, less stressful, and easier to manage.

User Research

We conducted semi-structured interviews with six college students to understand how they currently plan trips and what challenges they face. The participants included both undergraduate and graduate students with different levels of travel experience.

Our research showed that students often depend on multiple websites and apps to compare flights, hotels, and transportation options. This makes planning time-consuming and mentally tiring. Group travel creates additional difficulty because users must coordinate different budgets, schedules, and preferences.

Key Problems

  • Switching between multiple travel platforms
  • Slow and unstructured group decision-making
  • Difficulty comparing cost and time trade-offs
  • Planning feels stressful and overwhelming

User Needs

  • A single place to compare travel options
  • Clear cost and time information
  • Better support for group planning
  • Simpler and more guided workflows

Storyboards

Simple Task

Budget Trip Planning

A student searches for travel options within a fixed budget, compares available plans, and saves the best option.

Moderate Task

Comparing Travel Options

Users compare multiple travel options and evaluate trade-offs between cost and travel time before making a decision.

Complex Task

Itinerary and Events

Users manage their itinerary, explore nearby events, compare options, and add selected activities to create a complete travel plan.
